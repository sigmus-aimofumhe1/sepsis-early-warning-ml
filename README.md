# Autonomous Clinical Decision Support System: Predictive Analytics for Early Sepsis Warning

## Executive Framework & Strategic National Importance
Sepsis is a catastrophic systemic response to infection that stands as one of the leading causes of mortality in modern American clinical medicine. This project engineers a scalable digital health infrastructure designed to address critical operational and economic strain vectors across the United States healthcare sector:

* **The Mortality Crisis:** Sepsis contributes to **one-third (approx. 350,000 adult deaths annually) of all U.S. hospital fatalities**, outstripping many other acute conditions combined.
* **The Economic Burden:** Sepsis represents the **single most expensive condition treated in U.S. hospital networks**, draining over **$62 Billion annually** in systemic expenditures, heavily impacting federal Medicare and Medicaid safety nets.
* **Federal Alignment:** This software framework directly supports the statutory objectives of the **Federal SEPSIS Act** and implements real-time physiological decision boundaries designed to meet the **CDC’s Hospital Sepsis Program Core Elements** and Centers for Medicare & Medicaid Services (CMS) strict quality reporting mandates.

By shifting clinical intervention windows from *reactive treatment* to *predictive risk stratification 4 to 6 hours before onset*, this pipeline provides an open-source, multi-center blueprint for reducing critical care bed-locking and optimizing domestic hospital resource allocation.

## Multi-Center Data Infrastructure & Architecture
To ensure the pipeline generalizes across independent demographics and equipment ecosystems nationally, the models are trained on an aggregated multi-center clinical sandbox of **over 40,000 real longitudinal ICU stays** spanning two distinct geographical health systems:
1.  **Cohort A:** Beth Israel Deaconess Medical Center (Boston, MA)
2.  **Cohort B:** Emory University Hospital (Atlanta, GA)
